New York

When was it

I left France to go to the United States, to New York exactly, in September 2019. I came back five months later, in February 2020.

What did I do there

I spent five months in New York on an international campus, to take English classes. I organised this trip thanks to an agency, EF(Education First), that specialises in sending foreigners abroad to learn another language. To be honest, I didn't really need to take English classes given that my level had skyrocketed already after spending a full year in an English-speaking country, even though there was still lots of room for improvement of course(and there still is). However it was one way to stay for some time in the US without any problem.

So I had daily classes, from Monday to Friday, all dealing with different topics. There was one about crime and psychology for example, another one about New York's culture, one where we would discuss a given subject for an hour etc. I also had a main teacher, nearly everyday, who oriented his teaching towards speaking and debating mainly, as he judged that that was the most appropriate way to develop our ability to speak the language even further, given the overall level of English of the class I was in, which was pretty advanced. We did some grammar and vocabulary focused exercises, too.

Apart from the classes, the campus had in place clubs, organised at night usually, to which students could attend to take part in activities. The one I always went to was the TED-x club, every monday night. It was a great way to enhance my ability to be comfortable when speaking English, as many of the exercises that we did during each session involved speaking in front of others. It was all done in a very friendly, relaxed spirit, which made it very enjoyable.

There was also a bar on the campus, where students could go to have a drink and grab something to eat. That was the place where parties where organised, every once in a while.

And of course, I visited New York. The campus was actually located in Tarrytown, but the city that never sleeps was only one train ride away. I got to see the Statue of Liberty,

the Rockefeller Center, Times Square obviously,
the Brooklyn bridge,
the 9/11 memorial, and all the very touristic but still awesome places of New York. I didn't exactly visit the other boroughs, as Manhattan had plenty to offer, and I wasn't planning on trying to visit everything anyway.

One of the best things about that trip was that, given the time of the year where I was there, I had the opportunity to experience four big celebrations in the US: Halloween, Thanksgiving, Christmas, and New Year's Eve. Halloween is on another level there: big parades, everyone dressing up, people decorating their house and front yard, it was awesome to be part of it. I was also given the amazing opportunity to celebrate Thanksgiving in an actual American family, taste the food that they eat typically for the occasion, and get a bit more of an intimate insight into the American culture, for which I am extremely grateful. Christmas does feel magical in New York, with all the illuminations, the shows, the huge and fully decorated Christmas trees, the snow...Once again something that I will never forget. Finally, New Year's Eve is just a huge party in New York City. My friends and I took it to a nightclub close to Times Square, but the party vibe could be felt everywhere, and that was awesome.

Why did I do that trip

First of, after Australia, I knew that travelling was now part of my life, and that another trip had to happen in order to satisfy that need for adventure. So I thought about where I wanted to go, and how I could make another long trip happen again.

Australia didn't only spark my love for travels, it also made me realise how much I enjoyed speaking English. From there, I searched on Google "language trip", and I remember clicking on the EF link, reading about studying English abroad for a few months, the kind of programs they had to offer, and just like that, I knew what that next adventure was going to be.

Now, why New York. Well, simply because I had always wanted to go there. Having spent my childhood watching Marvel movies, watching Spider-Man swing his way through the streets of that city, I had developed a virtual bond with the Big Apple. I wanted to have enough time to visit and explore it, and the EF program was perfect for that.

Being part of a program like that one was also a sure way to meet lots of people, which is also why I wanted to travel abroad again. Travelling is such a rich experience when it comes to encounters, bonding with strangers from all over the world, and making friends for life. Although I have great friends back home and my family, I will never get enough the very unique, intense and genuine pleasure that comes from sharing a drink or having a nice chat that may turn into a lifetime friendship with foreigners.
